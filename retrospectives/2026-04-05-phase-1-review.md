# Phase 1 Review

Date: `2026-04-05`

Scope:

- article sample: `2026-03-16` to `2026-04-05`
- source data: `publishing-tracker.md`, `article-state/articles/*.json`

## What Was Verified

- A clear direction has already emerged: direct decision-oriented AI writing performs better than pure technical explanation.
- The account does not yet have a stable repeatable growth pattern, but it does have one strong validated content template.
- Most articles are still in a low-start range, so the stage is best understood as directional validation rather than broad product-market fit.

## Strong Signals

### 1. One article clearly ran

The strongest sample in this stage is:

- `2026-03-28：有条件就尽量用最顶级的大模型`

Observed outcomes:

- reads: `4175`
- shares: `46`
- reader feedback count: `17`

What matters is not only that it was opened, but that it triggered sharing and response. This makes it the clearest validated direction in the current sample.

### 2. One article completed an effective start

The second-tier sample is:

- `2026-04-01：为什么我开始更多用 Codex，而不是 ChatGPT`

Observed outcomes:

- reads: `156`

It did not break out, but it did prove that first-person workflow migration and real usage positioning can earn attention and strengthen account identity.

### 3. Technical-explanation-heavy posts are weaker at startup

Topics like:

- files
- bash
- memory
- limiting AI behavior

currently look more like “important technical thoughts” than “reader decisions that must be made now.” That makes them harder to start in a公众号 environment.

## Working Hypothesis

The current strongest-performing content pattern is:

- the topic is AI
- the payload is a direct decision or judgment
- the reader can immediately map the post to their own tooling, workflow, or cost choice

Short version:

`Help readers make a choice, not just understand a concept.`

## What Seems To Work

- direct judgment titles instead of descriptive topic titles
- content that changes a reader’s decision standard
- first-person usage framing when it leads to concrete positioning
- posts that are easy to argue with, agree with, or extend from personal experience

## What Still Looks Weak

- technical infrastructure explanation without immediate reader consequence
- posts whose value is clear only after reading several paragraphs
- topics that matter to engineers but are not translated into ordinary-user stakes

## Current Risks

- overfitting too hard to one breakout article
- mistaking technically strong writing for distribution-ready writing
- drifting into “interesting AI commentary” instead of building a recognizable decision-oriented account identity

## Operating Conclusion

For the next stage, prioritize content that answers:

- what should I choose
- what should I stop doing
- what matters more than people think
- where is the real tradeoff

Deprioritize content whose main value is:

- explaining a technical building block
- describing a system without a direct user decision attached

## Questions To Revisit Later

- Can the strong pattern behind `2026-03-28` be repeated across several more posts?
- Are “workflow migration” posts consistently second-tier winners?
- Can technical posts perform if they are rewritten as decision posts instead of explanation posts?

## Next Stage Focus

Prioritize these topic families:

- model and tool choice
- AI-era work judgments
- control, boundaries, and delegation

Track whether future strong posts continue to share these traits:

- direct title
- strong decision payload
- immediate relevance to the reader’s actual choices
