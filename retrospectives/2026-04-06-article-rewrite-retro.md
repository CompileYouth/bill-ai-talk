# 2026-04-06 Article Rewrite Retro

Article:

- `2026-04-07：AI 省下了生产时间，却放大了治理成本`

## What Went Wrong

1. I kept converging too early.
   I repeatedly compressed the piece into a short judgment before the argument had actually been explained. That produced several drafts that stated the conclusion but did not earn it.

2. I treated a working sentence as a finished article.
   Once the core judgment became clear, I moved too fast from “good sentence” to “publishable piece”. The result was a string of smart-sounding summaries instead of a complete article.

3. I let packaging drift away from the article hierarchy.
   The first image used an example as the main visual, then the next image repeated the article’s core point, and image placement broke the body’s argument order. This created confusion instead of reinforcement.

4. I reacted to each correction locally instead of re-evaluating the whole piece.
   That made the process feel like patching, not rewriting.

## What Was Missing

- a hard distinction between:
  - core judgment
  - supporting example
  - explanation layer
  - packaging layer
- a minimum bar for “clear enough to publish” on explanation-heavy short judgment posts

## Working Conclusion

For this account, a short judgment post still needs to fully explain one thing. If the topic requires:

- a real example to establish the tension
- one conceptual distinction
- one consequence layer

then stopping at 500-700 Chinese characters is usually too early.

## Rules To Follow Next Time

1. Do not compress to a final short draft until the article has:
   - one concrete example
   - one explicit explanation layer
   - one clean closing judgment

2. Do not treat a strong sentence as a finished article.

3. When the user asks to rewrite, rebuild the whole article around the final judgment instead of editing paragraph by paragraph.

4. For article images:
   - keep example and core judgment separate
   - do not repeat the same meaning across multiple images
   - do not place an image before the body has reached that idea
